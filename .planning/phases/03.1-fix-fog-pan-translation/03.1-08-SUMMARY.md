## Decisions Made

- **Implementation: Option (b)** — `canvas.translate(-canvasOffset.dx, -canvasOffset.dy)` at top of `_FogPainter.paint()`. Rejected Options (c) symmetric shift on drawRect bounds (brittle if future paint ops are added) and (d) over-draw rect by `max(|canvasTx|, |canvasTy|)` (wastes fragment-shader work). Option (b) makes the entire painter operate in a known coordinate frame with one line.
- **Defense-in-depth retention of the Plan 03.1-05 clip-path shift** — even though `computeFogClipPath(canvasOffset: ...)` is technically redundant after Plan 03.1-08's canvas.translate, removing it would couple FOG-12's correctness to FOG-13's translate ordering. The two compensations compose correctly because clipPath operates on path geometry (unaffected by subsequent canvas.translate) and canvas.translate operates on the prevailing transform stack (affects subsequent drawing operations).
- **Always-translate (no identity special-case)** — production code calls `canvas.translate(-canvasOffset.dx, -canvasOffset.dy)` unconditionally. canvas.translate(0, 0) is a documented dart:ui no-op; keeping the call sequence invariant simplifies the production code path and the test assertions.
- **drawRect-bounds assertion omitted from FOG-13 test (Rule 3 deviation)** — FogLayer's null-shader test seam (Plan 03-05) prevents `canvas.drawRect` from being called in widget tests. The FOG-13 invariant is preserved through the translate-BEFORE-restore call-order assertion (drawRect, when invoked in production with a non-null shader, lands inside the clip+translate block by source construction).

## Comparison vs Walk #2 Sub-section C Evidence

Walk #2 sub-section C captured the regression with quantitative evidence:
- 243 of 267 fog_transform JSONL rollups had `|canvasTx| > 100` or `|canvasTy| > 100`.
- Worst sustained `(canvasTx, canvasTy) = (+757.35, +319.46)` for the final ~50 seconds of the walk.
- Developer's verbatim verdict: *"Mirk shader is somehow rotating and not covering the whole map when zooming in... mirk made worse (mirk offset itself leaving part of the map revealed at the edges)"*.
- Screenshot: top-right + bottom-right diagonal strips of un-fogged street map visible at high zoom.

The Plan 03.1-08 fix mechanically prevents this symptom:
- Pre-fix: at `(canvasTx, canvasTy) = (+757, +319)` the rect at `Offset.zero & size` paints from screen coordinate `(757, 319)` to `(757+390, 319+844)` — i.e., almost entirely off-screen on a 390 × 844 viewport. The visible portion is shifted by exactly `+canvasOffset` from its intended position; the OPPOSITE-edge of the viewport is uncovered.
- Post-fix: `canvas.translate(-757, -319)` is applied BEFORE `drawRect`, so the prevailing transform stack now has `(MobileLayerTransformer's +757) + (our -757) = 0` net translation. `drawRect(Offset.zero & size)` then paints from screen coordinate `(0, 0)` to `(390, 844)` — full viewport coverage as intended.
- The CI gate `fog_rect_viewport_coverage_test.dart` reproduces Walk #2's exact magnitude `(757.35, 319.46)` and asserts the post-translate call sequence — any future regression that drops the `canvas.translate` call (or moves it to the wrong position in the call sequence) trips the test.
