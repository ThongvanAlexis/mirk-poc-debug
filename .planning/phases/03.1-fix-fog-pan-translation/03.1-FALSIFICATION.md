For 7 of the first 8 seconds (1777752640..1777752647), `canvasTx`/`canvasTy` did sit at exactly 0 (matching RESEARCH §Pitfall D's claim). But after second 1777752648, the Canvas transform jumps to `(5.035, -44.198)` and STICKS there for the remainder of the session — **MobileLayerTransformer DOES apply non-zero Canvas-level translation under some conditions** (likely related to map rotation, zoom step, or initial-load ordering). One sample at `(-31.36, ?)` and another at `(-5.17, +0.04)` show transient Canvas-translation jumps mid-session. This invalidates the Plan 03.1-02 fix's load-bearing assumption that `pixelOrigin / size % 1.0` gives the shader the correct offset; the actual offset reaching the painter's drawn pixels is `Canvas.transform * (pixelOrigin / size)`.

#### Finding 2: `pixelOriginX` jumps in massive discrete steps tied to zoom changes

```
epochSecond 1777752640..1777752647: pixelOriginX ~= 1.06e6   (zoom level ~13)
epochSecond 1777752648:             pixelOriginX ~= 1.67e6   (mid-zoom transition)
epochSecond 1777752649..1777752663: pixelOriginX ~= 1.88e6   (zoom level ~14)
epochSecond 1777752664..1777752675: pixelOriginX ~= 4.26e6   (zoom level ~15-16)
```

The pixelOrigin scales by `2^zoom` — at zoom 16, pixelOrigin magnitude is ~4.3 million. The `% 1.0` modulo applied to `pixelOriginX / size.width` (size.width ~390 px) yields a fraction in `[0, 1)` mathematically, BUT — for `pixelOriginX = 4255934.927218`, `pixelOriginX / 390 = 10912.65...`; `10912.65 % 1.0 = 0.65` — the floating-point precision loss in the high-magnitude division-then-modulo is meaningful. Worse: between consecutive paints during a single gesture, pixelOriginX may move by 1-100 pixels, but the modulo can wrap from 0.99 to 0.01 at any point in the gesture, producing a **single-frame discontinuity** that the shader applies as a hard seam.
