**Primary finding for Plan 03.1-10:** the markers cluster around inter-marker pan distances comparable to a small-integer multiple of `tilePeriodPixels`, consistent with the FALSIFICATION-3 mechanistic interpretation that B-3 wraps fire at noise-tile-period frequency. The user's "every x amount of pan you get a hard step" is empirically anchored at x ≈ 1-4 × 16.25 raw px = 16..65 raw px per perceived step. **This walk specifically did NOT exercise the high-pixelOrigin / extreme-zoom regime** — pixelOriginX magnitude stayed at ~1.064M (not the 4.26M Walk #2 captured), so the fp32-precision-degradation candidate (FOG-17a) is **neither corroborated nor contradicted** here; the stepping observed in this walk is wrap-discontinuity stepping at moderate zoom, not precision-induced sub-tile jitter at extreme zoom.

## Findings

### Step periodicity in pixelOrigin space

Inter-marker pan magnitude (`|dPxOrigin| = sqrt(dPxX^2 + dPxY^2)`) across the 7 marker-to-marker intervals:

- min = 6.93 raw px (markers 3→4 — barely above the tilePeriod)
- median = 39.01 raw px
- mean = 39.25 raw px
- max = 66.23 raw px (markers 4→5 — strong N-S pan)

In tile-period units (`/16.25`):
- min = 0.43 wraps
- median = 2.40 wraps
- mean = 2.42 wraps
- max = 4.08 wraps

**Interpretation.** The user perceived ~one hard-step event per 0.4..4 tile periods of camera pan, with ~2-3 tile periods being typical. This is consistent with FALSIFICATION-3 sub-section B's mechanistic prediction: B-3 wraps fire at noise-tile-period frequency, and the user's reaction-latency-bounded marker tap captures the wrap event ~within 1-3 wrap cycles after each one fires. The lone outlier at 0.43 wraps (markers 3→4) likely reflects two distinct steps perceived rapidly in succession (~3.4 s apart with the user re-tapping after a brief continuation of pan). Note also that the perceived stepping does **not** require a large pan magnitude — marker 4 fired after only 6.93 raw px of net camera motion (less than half a tile period), suggesting either the user pre-ran a large pan and reached marker 4 mid-deceleration, or the wrap event is perceptible even at sub-tile inter-marker net motion when the wrap fired within the rollup-second window.

### Correlation with zoom transitions

**None observed.** All 8 markers fall within a 112 raw-px-wide pixelOriginX band (1063847..1063959) — the entire walk happened at a single zoom level, no zoom-step discontinuities. This stands in stark contrast to Walk #2 which captured 0.5M-3.3M-pixel single-second pixelOrigin jumps from zoom transitions. **The user's perceived stepping is therefore NOT zoom-transition-correlated** — it fires during pure pan at a single zoom, exactly as the B-3 wrap-event mechanism predicts.

### Correlation with sdf rebuild bursts

`intersectingDiscCount = 2` for every SDF rollup in the walk window. `rebuildCount/sec` at marker seconds: 11, 5, 3, 12, 48, 21, 21, 10 (mean 16.4, max 48). The session-window-wide `rebuildCount/sec` distribution (across all 18 SDF rollups in the window): 1, 1, 22, 11, 38, 5, 38, 3, 29, 12, 33, 48, 15, 21, 21, 24, 33, 10. **PERF-08 cache-thrash pattern persists** even at this low pan-magnitude regime (max 48 rebuilds/sec at marker 4 with only 6.93 raw px of net camera motion → cache thrash is happening at sub-tile pan velocity, not just zoom-transition seconds). Per-rebuild cost stays at p95 8..14 ms, max 10..14 ms — same regime as Walk #2.

The relationship between SDF rebuilds and the perceived steps is **plausibly aggravating but not causal**: the cache invalidates per-paint during gesture even when the camera barely moves (the bbox quantisation grid is too tight); the SDF re-rasterisation produces a fresh ui.Image each rebuild; that ui.Image is then sampled by the shader through `texture(uSdfTexture, ...)`. If the SDF sampler picks up sub-quantised re-rasterisation jitter on top of the B-3 wrap discontinuity, the perceived step magnitude could be amplified. Plan 03.1-10 should consider whether FOG-17 (world-coordinate noise) is sufficient on its own, OR whether PERF-08 widening (1e-3 lat/lon, or disc-set-only cache key) is a necessary co-fix to fully eliminate the perceived stepping.

### Frame budget impact (PERF-07 retention)

Across the marker seconds (and the full 33-second walk window):
- `medianMs`: 0.148..0.405 (well under 16 ms budget; ~40-110× headroom)
- `p95Ms`: 0.259..8.416 (well under 32 ms budget; ~3.8-120× headroom)
- `maxMs`: 0.491..11.251 (well under 48 ms budget; ~4.3-100× headroom)

**PERF-07 retained.** No frame-budget pressure correlated with marker observations. The stepping is **not** a frame-time issue; it operates at the shader-sampling / coordinate level, exactly as FALSIFICATION-3 sub-section B predicted. This corroborates Walk #2's PERF-07 baseline (medianMs max 2.97; this walk's max-of-medians 0.405 is even lower).

### Rotation evidence (limitation: rollup doesn't capture rotation directly)

**`canvasTxMin/Median/Max` and `canvasTyMin/Median/Max` are identically 0.000000 for every one of the 33 fog_transform rollups.** Rotation was NOT disabled in this build (per task brief: build `79ab978` did not include UX-02). The fact that `canvasTx/Ty` is identically zero everywhere means MobileLayerTransformer applied no canvas translation during this walk — which on the FALSIFICATION-3 sub-section C mechanism implies the user kept the gestures pure-pan (no pinch-zoom-rotate that would induce non-identity matrix[0..5] OR matrix[12,13]).

Note the rollup schema gap: FogTransformLogger only captures `canvasTransform[12]` (Tx) and `[13]` (Ty), not the rotation slots `[0,1,4,5]`. So even if the user HAD rotated, this walk's rollup would not directly report rotation magnitude — only the indirect signature `canvasTx + canvasTy` magnitude (which is itself zero here). Plan 03.1-10's instrumentation extension (if rotation handling becomes a primary fix axis) should add a `canvasRotationDeg = atan2(matrix[1], matrix[0]) * 180/pi` field; FALSIFICATION-3's recommended FOG-16/UX-02 disable-rotation path makes that instrumentation unnecessary.

**Bottom line:** Walk #3b's marker observations are anchored to a **rotation-free, single-zoom, low-pan-magnitude** subset of the symptom space. The stepping mechanism observed here is the B-3 wrap-event mechanism only; the canvas-rotation symptom (FALSIFICATION-3 sub-section C) is **not exercised** in this walk and is **not** part of the marker-correlation evidence.

## Implications for Plan 03.1-10

### FOG-17 (world-coordinate-noise rewrite) — anchored evidence

The marker-to-marker pan distance distribution (median 2.4 wraps, mean 2.4 wraps, max 4.1 wraps per marker — i.e., user perceives a step every 1-4 tile periods of pan) is **direct empirical evidence** that the B-3 partial fix's wrap-frequency-shift to ~16 raw px is sub-perceptible-threshold-CROSSING (the user can resolve a step at 0.43-tile-period pan with a 6.93 raw-px net delta — that's a single wrap event, perceived). This justifies the world-coordinate approach: the cure is to eliminate `fract()` entirely, not to push `tilePeriodPixels` even smaller (which would just increase wrap frequency further, eventually below visual flicker threshold but at the cost of even-more-visible aliasing across the wrap boundary). The user's intent-correct mental model from FALSIFICATION-3 — *"I should see a new area of the shader"* — is the world-coordinate approach.

### FOG-17a (CPU-side integer/fractional decomposition) — anchored evidence

**Walk #3b is silent on FOG-17a** because the entire walk happened at pixelOriginX ~ 1.064M (zoom 13-ish — not the 4.26M / zoom 16 regime Walk #2 captured). At ~1.064M:
- fp32 ULP ≈ 0.127 raw px (1.064M × 2^-23)
- fract precision after dividing by tilePeriod (16.25): ≈ 0.0078 uv units

This is borderline — sub-tile-cell jitter of ±0.0078 uv at maxScale=24 would be ±0.19 cell-grid units, potentially perceptible. But Walk #3b cannot **distinguish** wrap-discontinuity stepping from precision-jitter stepping at this magnitude regime. Walk #2's 4.26M pixelOrigin observation gave fp32 ULP ≈ 0.51 raw px, fract precision ≈ 0.031 uv units — definitely perceptible.

**Recommendation:** FOG-17a remains motivated by Walk #2's high-zoom evidence (FALSIFICATION-3 sub-section D row D-1a). Plan 03.1-10 should **pair** FOG-17 with FOG-17a as a precision-mitigation defense-in-depth — but Walk #3b's empirical anchor is at the moderate-zoom regime, where pure FOG-17 (without 17a) might already eliminate the stepping. A staged approach (land FOG-17 first; re-walk at moderate AND extreme zoom; add 17a only if extreme-zoom regime still steps) is empirically justified.

### FOG-16 (rotation handling) / UX-02 (disable rotation)

Walk #3b provides **no direct evidence** for or against the rotation-correlated fog-cover failure mode (canvasTx/Ty identically zero throughout). FALSIFICATION-3 sub-section C's verbal evidence (developer's *"happens when you zoom and rotate"*) and developer-endorsed mitigation (*"disable rotation for now... simplier"*) stand unchallenged. The marker-correlation evidence here is orthogonal to the rotation case.

**Recommendation:** Treat UX-02 (disable rotation) as a Plan 03.1-10 scope-reduction independent of FOG-17, with no marker-evidence dependency. The marker analysis has nothing to add or subtract on the rotation axis.

### Test design implications

For the FOG-17 CI test (synthetic noise-stability metric proposed in FALSIFICATION-2 sub-section D row B-0 and FALSIFICATION-3 D-1):

- **Synthetic pixelOrigin trajectory should match the empirical marker-window pattern.** Specifically:
  - Mean pan velocity in marker windows: ~12 raw px/s (mean 39.25 raw px / mean 3258 ms ≈ 12.0 raw px/s)
  - Trajectory should span ≥7 tile-period crossings (mean 2.4 wraps × 8 markers ≈ 17 wraps; conservatively 7+ for a single test scenario)
  - Total trajectory magnitude: at minimum 5 × tilePeriodPixels = 81.25 raw px (sufficient to cross 5 wrap boundaries)
  - **At pixelOriginX magnitudes representative of moderate AND extreme zoom**: include 1.064M (this walk's regime) AND 4.26M (Walk #2's regime) as parameterized test scenarios.

- **Stability metric: per-pixel noise-output delta between consecutive synthetic frames** along the trajectory. The pre-fix (B-3 status quo) baseline should show wrap-discontinuity spikes (e.g., per-pixel delta jumping from ~0.001 typical to >0.5 at wrap-event frames); the post-fix (FOG-17 world-coordinate) trajectory should show monotonically smooth per-pixel delta (no spikes, no wrap-discontinuity signature).

- **Coverage threshold:** the test should fail if any consecutive-frame per-pixel delta exceeds a threshold tuned to be ~10× the typical inter-frame delta during smooth pan. Sample magnitudes from this walk's marker-second sample buffer (240 paints/sec × 1 second window) provide an empirical baseline for what "typical" looks like.

## Walk #3b Limitations

- **Rotation not exercised** — the user kept pan-only gestures despite rotation being enabled in the build; canvasTx/Ty identically zero throughout.
- **Single zoom level only** — pixelOriginX span 112 raw px; no zoom-transition seconds; FOG-17a (precision-degradation) hypothesis cannot be tested with this walk's data.
- **Short window** — only ~33 seconds of useful rollup data (24 seconds of marker activity); 8 markers — a small sample, but consistent enough to support a quantitative anchor for the median/mean wrap-magnitude finding.
- **No verdict signal** — this walk was supplementary, marker-only; no `approved`/`denied`/`iterating` from the developer; FALSIFICATION-3 already closed Walk #3 with `iterating: ITERATING-WITH-PARTIAL-PROGRESS-AND-ROTATION-REGRESSION` and this analysis only **anchors** that verdict with quantitative numbers, not changes it.
- **Rollup schema gap on rotation** — `FogTransformLogger` only captures matrix[12,13], not [0,1,4,5]; if rotation evidence were needed, this rollup would not directly provide it.
- **No verbal observation paired with each marker** — the user tagged each step but did not narrate WHICH visual feature they perceived (could be wrap-discontinuity, could be SDF re-rasterisation jitter, could be both compounded). The mechanistic interpretation here (wrap events) is inferential, anchored to the FALSIFICATION-3 mechanism documentation.
