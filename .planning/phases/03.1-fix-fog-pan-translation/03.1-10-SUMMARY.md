## Noise-Function Inspection Reference

Documented in `lib/presentation/widgets/fog_layer.dart` decomposition block + `lib/config/constants.dart` constant docstrings + `assets/shaders/atmospheric_fog.frag` comment block.

**Continuity invariant under FOG-17a integer wrap:** at the wrap boundary, the Dart-side decomposition shifts the shader's `uPixelOrigin` input by exactly `kPocFogIntegerWrapPeriodPx` (1536) raw pixels. The shader's `noiseUv = (fragUv * uResolution + uPixelOrigin) / kNoiseTilePx` therefore shifts by exactly `1536 / 384 = 4` noise grid units. Per-octave: `warped = noiseUv * uScale*` shifts by `4 * uScale*` per octave — `4 * 2.9 = 11.6`, `4 * 5.1 = 20.4`, `4 * 10.5 = 42.0`. `noise3` is periodic with period 1.0 in each of its three input axes (the hash3 lattice has period 1 by construction since `floor(p)` quantises to integer corners). `11.6`, `20.4`, `42.0` are NOT all integer-period offsets, so the FBM output IS NOT preserved under the integer-wrap shift in general for the rotated octaves.

**Resolution:** the wrap event produces a discontinuity in the FBM output at the wrap boundary — but this discontinuity IS already gated by the integer wrap period (~1536 raw pixels of camera pan) which at typical pan velocities (10-50 raw px/sec) corresponds to ~30-150 seconds of continuous one-direction pan. Walk #3b's empirical anchor: ~12 raw px/s mean pan velocity → integer wrap fires every ~128 seconds. By contrast, the pre-Plan-03.1-10 stepping fired every ~3 seconds (every ~16-65 raw px). FOG-17a moves the wrap from "every few seconds" to "every two minutes or more" — a perceptual reduction by a factor of ~40, sufficient for the Walk #4 verdict gate.

**If Walk #4 surfaces residual integer-wrap stepping:** Plan 03.1-12+ would adopt a more sophisticated decomposition strategy (e.g., a second wrap-period uniform that aligns with the FBM rotation matrices, OR pivot to a periodic-noise function — Worley/explicit-period Perlin — whose lattice IS preserved under integer shifts). Documented as the iteration path; not part of Plan 03.1-10's scope.
