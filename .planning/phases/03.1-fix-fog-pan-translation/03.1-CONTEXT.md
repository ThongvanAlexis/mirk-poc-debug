**Gathered:** 2026-05-02

<domain>
## Phase Boundary

Diagnose why the same-Canvas fog renders + rotates correctly with the camera but does NOT translate during pan, apply a fix, and re-validate the falsification criteria on iPhone 17 Pro. Phase 3 closed with `HYPOTHESIS DENIED` (Plan 03-08 walk 2026-05-01); Phase 3.1 either reverses that verdict to `CONFIRMED-AFTER-FIX` (unblocking Phase 4 + Phase 5) or strengthens it to `DENIED-FINAL` with deeper architectural diagnosis (terminating the POC per the original CONTEXT.md exit clause).

The architectural unknown to resolve: of the three diagnostic possibilities catalogued in `.planning/phases/03-fog-of-war-the-hypothesis/03-FALSIFICATION.md` ŌĆö (#1) `FogPainter` paints in screen-space without consuming the camera translation transform; (#2) `MobileLayerTransformer` applies translation at the widget layer (`Transform` widget) but the painter's local Canvas does not inherit it; (#3) camera updates do not propagate to `FogLayer` between pan-driven repaint cycles, so the painter holds a stale build-time `MapCamera` snapshot during the entire pan gesture ŌĆö which is the actual root cause, and what is the minimal-impact fix.

Out of scope for this phase: wisp particles (Phase 4 ŌĆö stays BLOCKED until 3.1 closes), final POC verdict promotion to repo root (Phase 5 ŌĆö also BLOCKED), pivoting to alternative renderers (`mapsforge_flutter`, custom MVT-on-Canvas, `flutter_gpu`) ŌĆö that decision is deferred to evidence-driven re-evaluation IF the in-`flutter_map` fix attempt fails, and is itself a Phase 3.2 conversation, not Phase 3.1 work, generalizing the regression test contract to wisps (Phase 4 discuss-phase decides), and any MirkFall port-back work (Phase 5 + project-close concern).

## Implementation Decisions

### Investigation strategy
- **Approach:** ship a single instrumented IPA that bundles BOTH (a) the most-likely fix attempt AND (b) full diagnostic instrumentation. One sideload UAT walk either confirms the fix works OR feeds back the diagnostic data needed to identify which of the three falsification possibilities is the real root cause. Highest information-per-walk; respects the ~4h sideload UAT cycle cost.
- **Primary fix-attempt choice ŌĆö DEFERRED to gsd-phase-researcher.** The researcher reads `flutter_map` 7.0.2 source for `MobileLayerTransformer`, the `MapCamera`/`MapController` event-stream plumbing, and the `CustomPaint` Canvas-transform inheritance behaviour, then recommends which possibility is the most-likely root cause and the minimal fix for it. Planner picks the fix from researcher findings.
- **Diagnostic instrumentation MUST disambiguate all three possibilities in a single walk.** Per-paint JSONL line capturing: `Canvas.getTransform()` matrix at `FogPainter.paint()` entry, the tile layer's transform at the same paint frame, `MapCamera.center` / `pixelOrigin` captured at `FogLayer.build()` time, frame counter, plus the diff matrix between the painter's Canvas transform and the tile layer's transform computed at paint time. Heaviest payload of the three options considered, picked deliberately so a single walk produces sufficient evidence even if the fix attempt fails.
- **Logging channel:** new dedicated `Logger('infrastructure.mirk.fog_transform')` hierarchy, sibling to the existing `infrastructure.mirk.frame_delta` and `infrastructure.mirk.sdf` loggers. 1-Hz rollup cadence to align with the other rollups (FrameDeltaProbe + SdfRebuildLogger) per Phase 3 CONTEXT.md log-timeline-alignment requirement ŌĆö post-walk grep correlates fog-transform diff against fog-paint frame-delta against SDF rebuild stats on the same per-second boundary.
- **Instrumentation lifecycle:** kept permanently in production code, always emitting at debug level (the log-timeline-alignment principle says future regressions ŌĆö in this POC OR in MirkFall after port-back ŌĆö surface immediately in the session log). Cost Ōēł 1 JSONL line per active second, negligible against the 50 MB log smoke-test ceiling. NOT gated behind `--dart-define` flags.

### Fix-failure contingency
- **Stop condition is DEFERRED until diagnostic evidence justifies the decision.** No hard timebox in walk count or calendar time. The `decide-after-diagnostic` policy: if a walk fails, the diagnostic logs from that same IPA reveal the root cause; the developer chooses among (a) `DENIED-final` and terminate the POC, (b) pivot Phase 3.1 (or spawn Phase 3.2) to retest the hypothesis on an alternative renderer such as `mapsforge_flutter` or a custom MVT-on-Canvas, or (c) keep iterating in `flutter_map` (walk source deeper, file an upstream issue, or fork) ŌĆö based on what the per-paint Canvas-transform-diff log actually shows.

<specifics>
## Specific Ideas

- **The diagnostic test that cleaves the three possibilities is documented verbatim in `03-FALSIFICATION.md`:** *"log Canvas.getTransform() in FogPainter.paint() vs MapCamera.center in FogLayer.build() during a pan gesture."* Phase 3.1's diagnostic instrumentation IS this test, instrumented for production sideload ŌĆö not a developer-machine debug-print exercise. The same JSONL log lines that disambiguate root cause also provide the long-term regression telemetry.
- **Possibility #3 (stale snapshot) is the leading hypothesis but NOT a locked decision.** Rotation works because rotation gestures may funnel through a different `setState`-triggering path than translation in `flutter_map` 7.0.2. The researcher must verify against actual `flutter_map` 7.0.2 source ŌĆö not against intuition ŌĆö before the planner commits to a fix shape.
- **`MobileLayerTransformer` is a load-bearing assumption that Plan 03-05's structural FOG-04 test treated as sufficient.** It is not. The augmented behavioural test must catch the case where MobileLayerTransformer is in the widget tree but does not propagate translation to the painter's Canvas. This is the lesson learned from Plan 03-08 made permanent in CI.
- **Single-MapCamera-snapshot discipline (FOG-07) cannot be violated by the fix.** If the fix is "read camera transform inside paint()", it must be the SAME snapshot that was captured at the top of `build()` ŌĆö re-reading `MapCamera.of(context)` from the painter would re-introduce the multi-snapshot anti-pattern (RESEARCH ┬¦Anti-Pattern 3 + Pitfall 10). The researcher should explicitly pin how their proposed fix preserves the FOG-07 invariant.
- **Identity `uSdfRect` cannot be touched by the fix.** RESEARCH ┬¦Anti-Pattern 1 ŌĆö dynamic `uSdfRect` re-introduces BUG-014 root cause. If a candidate fix is tempted to compensate for missing translation by varying `uSdfRect`, that's a regression ŌĆö flag and reject.
- **The 1-Hz log rollup cadence MUST stay aligned across SDF + frame_delta + new fog_transform loggers.** Post-walk grep correlates timelines on per-second boundaries; if the new logger drifts (e.g., probe-start-aligned instead of wall-clock-aligned), post-walk analysis becomes guesswork. Planner picks one cadence source (`Logger`-side `DateTime.now().millisecondsSinceEpoch ~/ 1000`) and the new rollup derives from it.
- **The Plan 03-08 verdict stands until the Phase 3.1 walk reverses it.** Until 3.1 produces a `CONFIRMED-AFTER-FIX` verdict, the formal POC architectural recommendation remains "do NOT port back to MirkFall as-implemented." Phase 3.1 either reverses this (and unlocks the migration) or strengthens it (and terminates the POC). Both outcomes are valid POC deliverables.
