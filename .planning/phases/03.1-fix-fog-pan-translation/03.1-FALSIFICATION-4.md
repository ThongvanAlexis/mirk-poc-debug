#### Interpretation against codebase

Two distinct phenomena observed simultaneously, both attributable to the **Web-Mercator pixel-space anchor** of the FOG-17 noise formulation:

**Phenomenon 1 — Numbers slide fast during zoom (the "scramble"):**

Plan 03.1-10's FOG-17 formulation `worldPx = fragUv * uResolution + uPixelOrigin; noiseUv = worldPx / kNoiseTilePx` anchors the noise pattern to **Web-Mercator world-pixel space**. `uPixelOrigin` doubles per zoom level (Web-Mercator pixel space scales `256 * 2^zoom` px wide). Consequence: fragments under any given screen position sample completely different noise positions per zoom step. Numerically — at zoom 13 a fragment at the blue-dot screen position sees `worldPx ≈ 1.064M`; at zoom 14 the same screen position sees `worldPx ≈ 2.128M`; at zoom 15 it sees `worldPx ≈ 4.26M`. Each zoom step doubles the world-pixel coordinate, which corresponds to a doubling of `noiseUv = worldPx / kNoiseTilePx` (since `kNoiseTilePx = 384.0` is a const). At `kNoiseTilePx = 384` raw px, a single zoom-out step crosses `1.064M / 384 ≈ 2770` noise tiles in coordinate space — i.e., the noise texture sampled at the blue dot shifts by ~2770 cells per zoom step. Visually: the noise pattern slides past the blue dot at extreme speed during pinch-zoom, exactly as the developer observed.

**Phenomenon 2 — Numbers stay the same size regardless of zoom level:**

`kNoiseTilePx = 384.0` is a shader-side const in **raw-pixel units**. Cells are 384 raw px on screen at every zoom level (~128 logical px on iPhone 17 Pro at DPR=3). The cells are **pinned in screen space**, NOT to ground meters. Zooming the camera does NOT enlarge the cells because the cells were never tied to ground in the FOG-17 formulation.

#### Mental-model gap diagnosis

The developer's intuition (*"the mirk 'center' isn't the blue dot of when we open the map, instead it must be a point outside of the screen and when zooming the shader is enlarged/shrinked relative to that point"*) is mechanistically off-by-a-step but pointing at the right structural problem: the noise pattern IS anchored to a fixed world-coordinate point — the **Web-Mercator world origin at the top-left of the global tile grid (worldPx = 0,0)** — NOT to the camera center, NOT to any geographic feature on the screen. As zoom changes, `uPixelOrigin = (camera position in Web-Mercator pixel space)` scales with `2^zoom`, and the blue dot's `worldPx = uPixelOrigin + uResolution/2` scales correspondingly. The user perceives this as "the noise pattern is enlarging/shrinking relative to a fixed off-screen anchor" — that fixed anchor is `worldPx = (0, 0)`.

The developer (correctly) expects fog-of-war to behave like ground features: at z=15 a "fog cell" should occupy ~1.2 km of ground (1210 m at lat 48.5°) and shrink visually when zooming out; at z=10 the same cell would occupy ~39 km of ground (32× bigger ground area on the map but same on-screen size? No — same ground area, smaller on-screen size; OR alternatively at z=10 a cell is `1024 m / metersPerPixel(z=10)` raw px on screen, smaller than at z=15). What the FOG-17 formulation actually produces: cells are 384 raw px on screen at every zoom level, but their **mapping to the ground varies** — at z=15 each cell maps to ~125 m of ground (`384 raw px × 0.32 m/raw_px ≈ 125 m`), at z=10 each cell maps to ~4 km of ground. Combined with the world-origin anchor of phenomenon 1, fragments under the blue dot sample completely different cells at different zoom levels.

#### Architectural decision crystallised — Fix B (world-meter anchor) selected

Four candidate fix axes (enumerated in `03.1-UAT-4.md` Sub-section D):

| #     | Fix axis                                                              | Pros                                                                                    | Cons                                                                                                                                                                                                                  |
| ----- | --------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Fix A | Anchor noise to camera position (NOT world origin)                    | Trivial change; noise stays pinned to camera centre across zoom                         | Noise stays the same shape regardless of camera world position — feels less "tied to the world"; under PURE PAN the noise scrolls because `uPixelOrigin` enters via the new offset term that's no longer zero        |
| **Fix B (PRIMARY — DEVELOPER-ENDORSED)** | **Anchor noise to fixed world-meter coordinates (zoom-invariant)**             | Noise anchored to a real geographic point; meter-scale; cells scale on screen with zoom (cells appear larger when zoomed in — exactly user's expected behaviour) | Requires a uniform `uMetersPerPixel` (slot 41 → 42; under shader budget cap); fragment-space noise frequency now scales with zoom — desired behaviour                                                                  |
| Fix C | Anchor noise to lat/lng directly                                       | Most "tied to the world" option; cells align with geographic features                   | cos(lat) scaling needs care for non-uniform cells near poles; per-fragment lat/lng computation is heavier than per-fragment world-meter computation; needs lat/lng decomposition Dart-side                            |
| Fix D | Accept zoom-anchored scramble; snap zoom to discrete integer levels    | Simplest from a math perspective                                                        | UX feels "step-zoom" instead of smooth pinch zoom; probably unacceptable for a hike-tracking POC                                                                                                                       |

**Fix B is selected** for Plan 03.1-12 (developer-endorsed during Walk #4 close-out). Trade-off summary:
