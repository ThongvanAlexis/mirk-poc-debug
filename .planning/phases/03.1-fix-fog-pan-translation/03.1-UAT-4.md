#### Mid-walk preliminary observation (in-progress — not the final verdict)

- **Q1 (stepping during pan):** developer's verbatim — _"this works for panning : perfect"_. FOG-17 world-coordinate noise sampling + FOG-17a CPU-side integer/fractional decomposition appear to have resolved the residual stepping symptom Walk #3 surfaced.
- **NEW failure mode surfaced — zoom-scramble (Q5, NOT a Walk #3 carry-over):** developer's verbatim — _"however zoom still scramble the mirk (rapid change of coordinate)"_. Under zoom-in/out gestures the noise pattern visibly scrambles. **Mechanism (preliminary, pending JSONL evidence):** Web-Mercator `camera.pixelOrigin` is zoom-dependent — incrementing zoom by 1 approximately doubles `pixelOrigin`. The Plan 03.1-10 FOG-17 formulation `worldPx = fragUv * uResolution + uPixelOrigin; noiseUv = worldPx / kNoiseTilePx` anchors the noise to PIXEL space, not geographic space. When zoom changes, `pixelOrigin` jumps by a delta that crosses many `kNoiseTilePx` periods at once → fragments sample completely different noise positions on consecutive frames → perceived as "scramble". The fix bundle was scoped to pan-stepping (Walk #3 Q1) and didn't account for zoom-step deltas. This is genuinely a new open question — **NOT** a regression of any prior fix and **NOT** a known Walk #3 failure mode. It needs an architecture-level decision: either anchor noise to a zoom-invariant coordinate (e.g., Web-Mercator world-meter or lat/lng-derived stable basis) OR snap zoom to discrete levels and accept a one-frame transition at each step OR scale the noise tile period inversely with the zoom-jump factor at the Dart call site.
- **Q2 (rotation-disabled):** developer's verbatim — _"rotation disabled confirmed"_. UX-02 holds walk-time. ✓
- **Q3 (reveal-hole-aligned):** developer's verbatim — _"revealed area working"_. FOG-12 retained walk-time. ✓
- **Q4 (fog-translates-with-camera):** implicit ✓ — "this works for panning" subsumes Q4 (if the noise pattern stayed pinned to the world-pixel origin during pan, the user would call it "smooth panning with no scrolling fog"; the term "perfect" carries the implicit Q4 confirmation).
- **Q5 zoom-scramble — refined mechanism (developer's debug-spiral observation):** developer's verbatim — _"when zooming it at maximum power blue dot is on the 84 square, when zooming out at maximum blue dot is at 92, but I don't think it's the 92 close to the 84, I think the issue is that the mirk 'center' isn't the blue dot of when we open the map, instead it must be a point outside of the screen and when zooming the shader is enlarged/shrinked relative to that point."_ This **confirms** the preliminary mechanism hypothesis with empirical detail: the noise pattern is anchored to `worldPx = (0,0)` (the Web-Mercator world origin at top-left of the global tile grid), not to the camera's world position. Under FOG-17 sampling `worldPx = fragUv * uResolution + uPixelOrigin`, every fragment's noise position scales with `uPixelOrigin`, which itself doubles per zoom level (Web-Mercator pixel space). The blue dot (camera center) is at `worldPx = uPixelOrigin + uResolution/2`. As zoom increases, `uPixelOrigin` doubles → the blue dot's `worldPx` doubles → fragments under the blue dot sample completely different noise grid cells per zoom level. Visually: the noise pattern appears to "enlarge/shrink relative to a fixed off-screen anchor (the world origin)" as zoom changes, exactly the user's intuition. **Architectural fix candidates** (post-walk decision pending):
    - **Fix A — anchor noise to camera position (NOT world origin):** redefine `worldPx = fragUv * uResolution + uOffset` where `uOffset = (-uResolution.x/2, -uResolution.y/2)` (so noise origin coincides with viewport center, i.e., the blue dot). Pros: trivial change; noise stays pinned to camera center across zoom. Cons: noise pattern stays the same shape regardless of camera world position — feels less "tied to the world" but more "tied to the viewport"; under PURE PAN the noise will scroll because uPixelOrigin enters via the `+ uOffset` term that's no longer zero (need to re-derive carefully).
    - **Fix B — anchor noise to fixed world-meter coordinates (zoom-invariant basis):** convert `pixelOrigin` to world-meter using Web-Mercator `metersPerPixel = 156543.03392 * cos(lat) / 2^zoom`, then sample noise in meter-space (`worldMeterX = pixelOriginX * metersPerPixel`). Pros: noise anchored to a real geographic point; doesn't change with zoom because meters per fragment scales identically. Cons: requires a uniform for `metersPerPixel` (currently 41 slots; budget impact); fragment-space noise frequency now scales with zoom (cells appear larger when zoomed in) — may be desired or not.
    - **Fix C — anchor noise to lat/lng directly:** sample noise at `worldUv = (lon - kPocInitialCameraLon, lat - kPocInitialCameraLat) / fixedDegreesPerCell`. Pros: the most "tied to the world" option; cells align with geographic features. Cons: cos(lat) scaling needs care for non-uniform cells near poles; per-fragment lat/lng computation is heavier than per-fragment world-meter computation; needs lat/lng decomposition Dart-side.
    - **Fix D — accept zoom-anchored scramble; freeze the camera's reveal pattern at the camera's current zoom by snapping zoom to discrete integer levels and re-anchoring noise on each zoom step:** simplest from a math perspective; UX feels "step-zoom" instead of smooth pinch zoom. Probably unacceptable for a hike-tracking POC.
