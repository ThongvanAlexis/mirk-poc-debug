#### Mechanism — `canvas.drawRect` paints in the canvas-translated frame

`_FogPainter.paint()` calls (lib/presentation/widgets/fog_layer.dart:466-469):

```dart
canvas.drawRect(Offset.zero & size, Paint()..shader = liveShader);
```

Plan 03.1-05's CANVAS-FRAME-ALIGNMENT fix shifted the **clip path** by `-canvasOffset` so the reveal-hole geometry stayed co-located with sibling layers. However, the **rect drawn for the fog cover** is still `Offset.zero & size` — which IS subject to the same canvas transform. When `canvasTx = +757.35`, the rect paints from screen coordinate (757, 319) to (757+390, 319+844) — that is, almost entirely off-screen. The visible portion of the fog cover is shifted by exactly `+canvasOffset` from its intended position, leaving the OPPOSITE-edge of the viewport uncovered by fog. Top-right + bottom-right strips visible matches a positive `canvasTx` + positive `canvasTy` translation.

This was anticipated as a non-trivial concern in the original Plan 03.1-05 triage:

- **Option (a) "less invasive but riskier":** shift the clip path by `-canvasOffset` so the reveal hole stays world-aligned. Riskiness: only addresses ONE of the two canvas-affected drawing operations (clip path); leaves `drawRect` paint subject to canvas transform.
- **Option (b) "more invasive but architecturally cleaner":** call `canvas.translate(-canvasTx, -canvasTy)` (or apply the inverse transform via `canvas.transform`) at the top of `paint()` so ALL subsequent drawing ops paint in the world-aligned screen frame. Compensates symmetrically for clip + rect.
- Plan 03.1-05 chose Option (a). Walk #2 empirically falsifies it for the rect-cover case.

#### Hypothesis for Plan 03.1-07 (FOG-13)

Either:
- **Option (b) revisit:** `canvas.translate(-canvasTx, -canvasTy)` at the top of `paint()` so the entire painter draws in the world-aligned frame; remove the `-canvasOffset` shift from `computeFogClipPath` (now redundant).
- **Option (c) symmetric shift:** shift BOTH `clipPath` AND `drawRect` bounds by `-canvasOffset`. The rect call site becomes `canvas.drawRect(Offset(-canvasTx, -canvasTy) & size, Paint()..shader = liveShader)`. Less invasive than Option (b); does not require removing existing clip-path shift.
- **Option (d) over-draw:** paint the rect with bounds that extend beyond the viewport in BOTH directions by `max(|canvasTx|, |canvasTy|)`; the extra paint is clipped by the device-screen bounds anyway. Cheap; works regardless of canvasTransform sign or magnitude.

The CI test FOG-12 (`fog_canvas_frame_alignment_test.dart`) currently asserts the **clip path bounds** shift by `-(canvasTx, canvasTy)`. It does NOT assert the **rect-cover bounds** are similarly compensated. Plan 03.1-07 must extend FOG-12 (or add FOG-13) to also assert the rect-cover bounds compensation.
