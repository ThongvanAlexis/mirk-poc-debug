### Diagnostic JSONL — fog_transform rollup (NEW for Phase 3.1 — `infrastructure.mirk.fog_transform`)

**36 1-Hz rollups** captured. **Three structural findings (the load-bearing diagnostic evidence):**

#### Finding 1 — `canvasTx`/`canvasTy` are NOT flat at 0

For the first 7 seconds (1777752640..1777752647), Canvas transform sat at `(0.000, 0.000)` matching RESEARCH §Pitfall D's claim. From second 1777752648 onward, the Canvas transform JUMPED to `(5.035, -44.198)` and held for the rest of the session. RESEARCH §Pitfall D was wrong: `MobileLayerTransformer` DOES apply non-zero Canvas-level translation under some conditions (likely related to map rotation or zoom step). This invalidates the Plan 03.1-02 fix's load-bearing assumption that `pixelOrigin / size % 1.0` alone gives the shader the correct offset.

#### Finding 2 — `pixelOriginX/Y` jumps in massive zoom-correlated steps

```
seconds 1777752640..1777752647: pixelOriginX ~= 1.06e6   (zoom ~13)
second  1777752648:             pixelOriginX ~= 1.67e6   (zoom transition)
seconds 1777752649..1777752663: pixelOriginX ~= 1.88e6   (zoom ~14)
seconds 1777752664..1777752675: pixelOriginX ~= 4.26e6   (zoom ~15-16)
```

At zoom 16, pixelOrigin magnitude ~4.3 million. The `(pixelOriginX / size.width) % 1.0` modulo applied at this magnitude introduces visible floating-point quantisation when the result is sampled multiple times per second.

#### Finding 3 — `uOffsetX`/`uOffsetY` oscillate full-range (0..1) within a single 1-Hz rollup during gestures

Sample rollup during pan (epochSecond 1777752641): uOffsetX ranges `0.0058..0.9925`, uOffsetY ranges `0.0109..0.9998`. The shader is receiving wildly different uOffset values across consecutive paints (~120 paints/sec during gesture). Each modulo wrap from 0.99→0.01 is a full-tile-width discontinuity in the noise sample coordinate — exactly what the developer described as "the seed of the mirk was changing".

Full extracts at `.uat-tmp/fog_transform.jsonl`.

### Subjective verdict (Criterion B)

Developer's verbatim verdict: **iterating with major progress** — fix is partial. Five observations:

1. **PERF-07 GREEN.** "Frame-delta probe `med` / `p95` / `max` all under 4 ms during the walk. Performance budget met."

2. **"Seed changing" failure mode.** "When pan or zoom, the mirk is quickly changing, like if the seed of the mirk was changing." Fog noise pattern not stable across frames during gesture; appears to regenerate / shimmer rather than translate as a stable pattern. Three candidate root causes: (a) modulo-1.0 wrap producing seam visibility, (b) noise sampled in screen-space rather than world-space, (c) noise function depending on a per-frame varying input. Diagnostic Finding 3 implicates (a) primarily.

3. **Partial pan tracking.** "Sometimes when panning it behaves like we want it to behave (move with the map)." Intermittent correct behaviour. Suggests timing race, zoom-level dependency, or gesture-state-dependent path. NOT a constant-zero failure (Plan 03-08 fully reproduced would be NEVER tracking) — uOffset derivation is reaching the shader at least sometimes. Diagnostic Findings 2 + 3 confirm the fix lands in the binary; the intermittent visibility correlates with high pixelOrigin magnitude AND rapid gesture velocity.

4. **Reveal-hole offset from blue dot.** "The revealed area is being offsetted from the blue dot during pan/zoom." Reveal hole and blue dot no longer co-located during gesture. Diagnostic Finding 1 confirms `canvasTx/Ty` jumps to `(5.035, -44.198)` mid-session — the SDF coordinate frame (lat/lon-derived) and the blue-dot CircleMarker frame end up in different Canvas frames once Canvas translation is non-zero, and they diverge by exactly that translation.

## Deviations / Surprises

- **RESEARCH §Pitfall D claim invalidated:** the document asserted Canvas at identity inside MobileLayerTransformer at rotation=0; reality (Finding 1) shows non-zero Canvas translation under conditions Plan 03-08 + Plan 03.1-02 + Plan 03.1-03 did not anticipate. This is exactly the kind of pre-walk-research-mismatch the FOG-10 diagnostic stream was authored to surface.
- **SdfCache thrash:** SDF rebuilds 12-115 times per second during gesture seconds despite constant disc count. The cache key includes viewport; viewport drifts per-paint during pan; cache invalidates per-paint. Per-rebuild cost stays sub-13ms so frame budget is fine, but the unintended cache-thrashing is itself a candidate contributor to observation 2 (per-frame regenerated SDF state may feed back into the shader's sample coordinate). Plan 03.1-04 RESEARCH should evaluate whether the cache key should drop the viewport component.
- **Session shape:** the walk was a gesture-stress session (~36 seconds) rather than a 5-minute outdoor walk. The shorter window still captured all four observation classes plus the diagnostic Findings; a longer outdoor walk after Plan 03.1-04 lands will produce additional pixelOrigin trajectory data for re-validation.
