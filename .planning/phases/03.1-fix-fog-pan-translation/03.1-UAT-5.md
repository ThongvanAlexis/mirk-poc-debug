**Mechanism (period-commensurability gap):** FOG-17a operates in PIXEL space (`kPocFogIntegerWrapPeriodPx = 1536 raw px`); FOG-18 noise tile operates in METER space (`kPocFogNoiseTilePxMeters = 1024 m`). The conversion factor `metersPerPixel = 156543.03 × cos(lat) / pow(2, zoom)` is zoom-dependent so the two periods are non-commensurate at every zoom level. At z=15 lat 48.535° each FOG-17a wrap shifts noise by `1536 × 3.162 / 1024 = 4.7421 cells` — a 0.7421-cell visible discontinuity. The same mechanism causes Q1 stepping during sustained pan AND Q5 noise scramble during zoom transitions. Both markers fired during Walk #5 correlate with Y-axis FOG-17a wrap events in the fog_transform JSONL within ±1 sec.

**Plan 03.1-14 path forward: Fix B′ — meter-space FOG-17a decomposition.** Compute `worldMeters = pixelOrigin × metersPerPixel` Dart-side. Decompose into `(integerMeters, fractionalMeters)` in METER space. Choose `kPocFogIntegerWrapPeriodMeters` as a multiple of `kPocFogNoiseTilePxMeters` (e.g., `4096 m = 4 × 1024 m`). Forward `bounded_meters` to shader. Wraps then occur at integer multiples of tile period → invisible discontinuity. Zoom-invariant because bounded_meters is geographic, not pixel. NEW v1 requirement candidate: **FOG-19** (integer/fractional decomposition operates in noise-anchor's coordinate space).
