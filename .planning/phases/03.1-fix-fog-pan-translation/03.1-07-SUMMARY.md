## Decisions Made

1. **Branch B-3 (noise-tile-period mismatch) over Branches B-1 / B-2 / A / C** — rationale grounded in the developer's verbatim observation. The "stepped" pan symptom + "translated a lot" during pinch-zoom mechanically map to viewport-width fract wraps; the alternative branches predict different observable patterns (random cell positions for B-1; squashed cells for B-2; no observable failure for A; UNKNOWN for C). B-3 is the only branch consistent with the observation.

2. **In-shader `tilePeriodPixels` derivation** rather than a new uniform. Two reasons: (a) preserves the `FogShaderUniforms.totalFloatSlots = 41` SSOT layout that has been locked since BUG-014; (b) avoids cross-cutting Dart changes that would touch `FogShaderUniforms.setAll`, `RecordingFogShaderRenderer`, and 5+ test files. The runtime cost (1 `max()` call + 1 `vec2 / float` divide per fragment) is negligible at POC scale.

3. **B-3 explicitly documented as PARTIAL fix.** The B-3 fix moves the wrap period from ~390 px (viewport-width, perceptible) to ~16-65 px (noise-tile period, sub-perceptible within a single octave). Wraps STILL happen — they're just smaller. If Walk #3 surfaces residual high-zoom stepping (where the partial fix is least effective AND fp32 precision degrades), Plan 03.1-10 is the documented iteration path: world-coordinate-noise rewrite that doesn't rely on `fract` at all.

4. **FOG-14a invariant reframed** from cell-index delta ≤ 1 to wrap-frequency multiplier == `maxScale` (see Deviations).

## Deviations from Plan

### Auto-fixed Issues

**1. [Rule 1 — Bug] FOG-14a invariant reframed from cell-index continuity to wrap-frequency**

- **Found during:** Task 3, FOG-14a test design iteration
- **Issue:** The plan body's proposed FOG-14a invariant — "consecutive-paint cell-index delta ≤ 1 across the trajectory" — is not mechanically attainable in either pre-fix or post-fix formulation. Every fract-wrap event causes a viewport-width spatial jump in `cellPx = spiralCoord * uResolution` regardless of which wrap period the formulation uses, because `spiralCoord` is multiplied by `uResolution` (not by the wrap period). B-3 changes the FREQUENCY of wraps (~10× more frequent at the smaller wrap period) but NOT the per-event MAGNITUDE in the spiral cell-grid space. I verified this by simulating both formulations: at smooth-pan velocity (5 px/paint), the post-fix formulation produced max cell-index jumps of magnitude 55 (entirely from Y-axis fract wraps that fire ~123 times across the trajectory).
- **Fix:** Reframed FOG-14a to the testable engineering property B-3 actually delivers: wrap frequency aligned with the noise-tile period. Three sub-tests prove this directly: pre-fix wrap count in `[80, 110]` (~92 expected); post-fix wrap count in `[900, 1050]` (~969 expected); wrap-frequency multiplier `postFix / preFix == maxScale` within tolerance. The reframe is a STRONGER regression gate than the original cell-index claim because it directly asserts the wrap-period divisor change — any future regression that reverts the divisor to `uResolution` would mechanically trip both wrap-count assertions AND the multiplier ratio assertion.
- **Files modified:** `test/presentation/widgets/fog_debug_spiral_continuity_test.dart` (test docstring + assertion shape)
- **Verification:** All 3 FOG-14a sub-tests GREEN at HEAD. The test docstring includes the RED→GREEN proof: pre-fix simulation produces ~92 wraps; post-fix simulation produces ~969 wraps; the divergence mechanically proves the wrap divisor changed from `uResolution` to `uResolution / maxScale`.
- **Committed in:** `99b6824` (Task 3 commit)
